About

Anjali Tripathi

I am a geopolitical and policy analyst working at the intersection of global affairs, governance, conflict, and development — with a growing specialisation in ESG, corporate sustainability, and the political economy of climate risk.

I hold a Master's in Conflict Management and Development from Banaras Hindu University, where my research focused on post-conflict migration, refugee policy, and gendered vulnerabilities through fieldwork with Rohingya communities in Delhi.

My experience spans policy research, political risk assessment, and governance analysis. I approach complex issues through structured, evidence-based frameworks — combining international relations theory, political economy, and development studies to produce decision-relevant insights.

I am particularly interested in how ESG frameworks intersect with geopolitical realities: how climate stress amplifies conflict, how governance deficits create ESG blind spots, and how supply chain politics reshapes corporate sustainability strategies.
